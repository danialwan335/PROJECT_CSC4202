# 4.1 Problem Definition

## 4.1.1 Background

In rural highland Malaysia, a post-landslide emergency creates a disrupted road network. Emergency response vehicles such as ambulances must navigate through surviving secondary roads — logging tracks, plantation roads, and forest paths — since the primary highway is blocked by debris.

Manual routing decisions under disaster conditions at night are slow, inconsistent, and potentially fatal. An algorithmic solution is required to compute the provably optimal (fastest-time) evacuation route in real time.

## 4.1.2 Problem Statement

Given a weighted undirected graph G = (V, E, W) representing the surviving post-landslide road network, where:
- V = set of road junctions and landmarks (vertices),
- E = set of surviving road segments (edges),
- W : E → ℝ⁺ = a function assigning a non-negative travel time in minutes to each edge,

and given a **source node s** (hospital) and **destination node d** (patient's village),

**find the path P* from s to d in G such that the total travel time Σ w(e) for all edges e in P* is minimised.**

This is the **single-source shortest path (SSSP) problem** on a non-negatively weighted undirected graph, solved optimally by **Dijkstra's Algorithm**.

## 4.1.3 Goal and Expected Output

- **Goal:** Minimise total ambulance travel time from hospital to patient's village.
- **Expected Output:**
  - The **sequence of nodes** (junctions/landmarks) forming the optimal route.
  - The **total minimum travel time** in minutes.
  - The **travel time for each segment** of the route.
